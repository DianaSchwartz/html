As the capital of Tatarstan Republic, Kazan is a glorious city that represents Russia's Tsarist past.
With complex palaces, towering churches, and a thriving cultural scene, Kazan is a pure Russian city with incredible beauty and heart.
As the "Third Capital of Russia," there is plenty to see and to do while staying in Kazan.
Visitors can enjoy long walks down the streets of the city, as each of them offers a new arrangement of colors and historic buildings.
Kazan is also one of the largest economic, political, scientific, educational, sport and cultural centers of the country.
The city lies at the confluence of the Volga and the Kazanka rivers, covering an area of 425.3 square kilometres, with a population of over 1.2 million residents, up to roughly 1.6 million residents in the urban agglomeration.
A bit of history
The city was founded more than 1,000 years ago by the Mongols (Tatars) of the Golden Horde after their overthrow of the Bulgar kingdom on the middle Volga. In 1438, Kazan became the capital of the Khanate of Kazan (Tatar Turkic state). In 1552, Kazan was captured by Ivan the Terrible and became a part of Russia.
After the dissolution of the Soviet Union, Kazan stayed as the capital of the Republic of Tatarstan. Kazan became one of the most important centers of the Russian Federation.
The city earned the right to host both the 2013 Summer Universiade, 2018 FIFA World Cup and also the 45th Worldskills Competition was held in Kazan on 2019.
The Best Things to do in Kazan, Russia:
  • Look at the locals walking along the pedestrian Street named by Bauman,
  • Get a culture hit at the Hermitage-Kazan Center,
  • Explore the Kremlin,
  • Gaze at the Kul Sharif Mosque,
  • Eat Tatar food,
  • Be inspired by the Temple of All Religions,
  • Visit the Soviet Lifestyle Museum,
  • Catch a match at the Kazan Arena,
  • Soak up the local atmosphere at the Central Market.

Kazan is one of the brightest examples of how people of different nationalities and religions live in one city peacefully, in an atmosphere of friendliness and tolerance.
Ethnic and religious diversity in the capital of Tatarstan has been preserved for more than one century.
The most spoken language in Kazan is Russian, and the Tatar language is the second most spoken.
The Republic of Tatarstan is headed by its own President, and the Republic also has its own national flag and symbol.
